Research Interests

From July 2014 I will be moving to Manchester Uni to join the labs of Rasmus Petersen and Mark Humphries.

I am interested in the processing that underlies active sensing. I feel that studying and replicating how the embodied brain encodes, and acts upon, the world is at the heart of understanding how the brain works, which is why my research is focussed on computational modelling and robotics. As part of the Sheffield Centre for Robotics (ScentRO), Active Touch Laboratory at Sheffield (ATL@S), Adaptive Behaviour Research Group (ABRG), BIOTACT, and EFAA I have been developing models of whisker based tactile perception, and robot technologies based on the rodent whisker system. I have also worked on a project (and patent) to develop a sensory augmentation device for providing distal touch information to the visually impaired (some details on this, and the whisker work, can be seen on a BBC Look North video here).
